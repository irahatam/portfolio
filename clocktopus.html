Dr. Clocktopus

[embedded systems & product management]

As college students, we struggle deeply with getting on a healthy sleep schedule. Many different alarm clocks exist that try to solve this problem, but they’re all gimmicks that don’t work; things like an alarm similar to a bathroom scale that you must stand on to turn off or having to solve a puzzle to turn off the alarm. Our idea is simple: an alarm clock that consists of two parts. One component is at your bedside like a normal alarm clock and the other component is the off button which is placed far away. There would be no way to turn off the alarm without having to get up and stay up. Our mission is to help people get on a healthy sleep schedule without the need for discipline.

We will apply the Agile method of extreme programming (technically extreme development) to test the product. Meaning the first part that must be done is the basic alarm component and then once that’s tested and confirmed to work, we will add features one at a time and continuously test each iteration of the product. This will allow us to rapidly build the device and quickly identify problems. Since this is an embedded systems project, product testing means making sure the inputs to the device produce consistent outputs. Towards the end of development, we will also stress test the product by entering edge and corner case inputs to make sure the quality of the device meets our expectations.

This is an on-going project, stay tuned for updates!
